ABOUT

Choke redistributes second-hand digital and analog music media by refashioning discarded CDs and LP records into small collections and making them available to the public.
It operates somewhat like a buisiness, but is not primarily driven by financial gain. Rather, a comprehensive list of Choke's motivations can be found below.
It will be up and running for one week (October 1st - October 7th). Any order placed within this timeframe is guaranteed to be processed and shipped. After which, it will become inactive and the following three months will be spent collecting, listening, and preparing music media for the next iteration of Choke.
Motivations
  • Many of these discarded items have enourmous artistic value. The goal is to identify as many of those as possible, acquire them, then redistribute the material back into individuals' homes.
  • Choke creates digital files of every item that enters its facility (RIPing in the case of CDs, and needle dropping in the case of LPs). This is because, aside from redistribution, it also sets out to amass the largest stockpile of audio samples in the Rio Grande Valley. Made available for artists to use at no cost whatsoever. More information on that will be provided later through Updates.
  • Optical discs and LP records are ingenius mediums humans have developed to store information. Preserving them is a cool way to acknowledge the trajectory our technology has developed in througout the decades.
  • We belive it's one of the best ways to discover new music. Yes, you can traverse the endless web of related artists on a given Spotify page; and yes, you can exhaust every possible group of any given sub-genre, but discovering with the complete and utter randomness of Choke grants the user a newfound freedom in music discovery that is all too rarely experienced with modern day algorithms designed to pick out music they think you'll like.
